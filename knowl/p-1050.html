TheoremÂ 4.3.6 is more powerful than it may seem. Although you have probably found prime factorizations before, you may not have thought much about the fact that they are unique. Consider the statement \(3\mid 2^{101}\text{.}\) Is this true or false? Well, since \(2^{101}\) is a prime factorization, the only prime divisor it has is 2. Thus, \(3\mid 2^{101}\) is false. If you have the prime factorization of an integer, then you know exactly which primes (and which powers of primes) divide the integer.
